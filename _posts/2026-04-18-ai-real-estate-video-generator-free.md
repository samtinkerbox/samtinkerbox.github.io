## How I Tested These Tools

My testing methodology was deliberately practical — I didn't just run demos. I came in with the actual content challenges a real estate professional faces:

**Test 1 — Listing narration:** I wrote a 200-word script for a fictional 3-bed/2-bath home in Austin, Texas, and pushed it through every tool to see what kind of video it produced. I evaluated voice quality, visual matching, pacing, and whether I'd be embarrassed to post it on Zillow.

**Test 2 — No-script workflow:** I gave each tool only the property address, a list of features (open plan kitchen, backyard pool, new roof), and five stock photos. This tested how much "thinking" the AI would do versus how much hand-holding it needed.

**Test 3 — Agent branding video:** I asked each tool to create a 60-second "meet your agent" intro video using either an AI avatar or a text-to-video approach.

**Test 4 — Free tier reality check:** I deliberately stayed within free plan limits to document what you actually get before you swipe a credit card.

I evaluated on five dimensions: output quality, ease of use, speed, free tier generosity, and real estate specificity (do they have templates or stock footage that actually looks like real estate?).

## 1. Fliki — Best Overall for Script-to-Listing Video

**What it does:** Fliki converts written scripts — or even blog posts — into narrated videos with AI voice, stock footage, and on-screen text. It's genuinely fast. I pasted my listing script and had a watchable video in under four minutes.

**What I liked:**

- **The voice quality is surprisingly good.** On my listing test, I used one of Fliki's "professional" voices and ran it past a colleague without telling her it was AI. She asked which voiceover artist I used. That doesn't happen often.
- **Stock footage matching is actually relevant.** When my script mentioned "spacious open-plan kitchen," Fliki surfaced kitchen footage without me manually searching. For real estate content specifically, this matters — you don't want a video about a luxury condo cutting to footage of a warehouse.
- **The blog-to-video feature is underrated.** If you write neighborhood guides or home-buying tips on your website, you can turn those into YouTube-ready videos in minutes. That's a content multiplication play most agents aren't thinking about.
- **Free plan lets you experiment without commitment.** You can create and preview videos for free, though exports are limited and watermarked on the free tier.

**What could be better:**

- The free plan watermark is prominent — you're not posting this to a client-facing listing without upgrading.
- Stock footage, while relevant, is still stock footage. For luxury listings, the generic visuals can feel mismatched with the property's actual aesthetic.
- You can't upload your own property photos on the free plan and have the AI animate or enhance them.

**Pricing:**

| Plan | Price | Key Limits |
|------|-------|------------|
| Free | $0 | Watermarked, limited minutes |
| Standard | $28/mo | 180 mins/mo, AI voices, HD export |
| Premium | $88/mo | Unlimited, premium voices, API access |

**Verdict:** For agents who want to produce professional-sounding listing narration videos quickly and cheaply, Fliki is the most practical free starting point I tested. The upgrade at $28/month is genuinely reasonable if you're doing more than 4–5 listings per month.

## 2. Pollo AI — Best for AI-Generated Property Visuals

**What it does:** Pollo AI is a newer AI video and image generation platform. For real estate specifically, it's interesting because it can generate realistic-looking property visuals from text prompts — think virtual staging, curb appeal mockups, and property visualization from descriptions. It also does text-to-video and image-to-video conversion.

**What I liked:**

- **Virtual staging through AI is genuinely useful here.** I uploaded a photo of an empty living room and prompted Pollo to fill it with "modern Scandinavian furniture, warm lighting, plants." The result was good enough to use as a supplemental visual in a listing video — not to deceive buyers, but to help them visualize potential.
- **Image-to-video animations** gave my static property photos a subtle "Ken Burns"-style motion that looks polished without being over-the-top. Real estate photographers charge extra for this kind of treatment.
- **Free credit tier is meaningful.** Unlike some tools that give you barely enough to see the loading screen, Pollo's free credits let me generate 8–10 real test outputs before hitting a paywall.
- **Fast generation.** Most outputs came back in under 60 seconds, which matters when you're trying to turn around a listing package in a day.

**What could be better:**

- The platform is still maturing. The UI has some rough edges and the documentation assumes you already know what you're doing with AI prompts.
- Generated property visuals, while impressive, can have the subtle "AI uncanny valley" problem — slightly wrong light switches, furniture legs that don't quite touch the floor. You need to review outputs carefully before client-facing use.
- Real estate-specific templates are sparse. You're mostly working with general video/image tools and applying them creatively to property use cases.

**Pricing:** Pollo uses a credit model. Free credits on signup, with paid plans starting around $10/month for light use.

**Verdict:** Pollo AI is the tool I'd recommend to agents interested in AI-generated staging visuals or animated listing photos. It's early-stage but the output quality for property visuals is ahead of where I expected it to be.

👉 [Try Pollo AI](https://pollo.ai?ref=nza3zdr)

**What it does:** ElevenLabs is a voice AI platform, not a video generator per se — but it belongs in this list because voiceover is often the single biggest quality gap in DIY real estate videos. ## FAQ

### Can I make real estate videos with AI for free?

Yes, genuinely — not just as a teaser. Fliki's free plan lets you create and preview complete listing videos. ElevenLabs gives you 10,000 characters of voiceover per month at no cost. Pollo AI provides free credits on signup. The main limitation is watermarks on free-tier exports, which you'll need to upgrade to remove for client-facing use. For internal mockups or social media where watermarks are less of an issue, free tiers are practically useful.

### What is the best AI tool to create real estate listing videos?

For most agents, Fliki is the best starting point because it handles the full pipeline — script input, AI voiceover, stock footage matching, and export — in a single tool. If you want higher voice quality, combining Fliki with ElevenLabs voiceover gives you a noticeably more professional result. For agents who want to appear on camera via an AI avatar, HeyGen is the best option.

### Can AI do virtual staging for real estate videos?

Yes, and this is one of the more exciting applications. Pollo AI can take a photo of an empty room and generate realistic furnished versions, which you can then animate and incorporate into listing videos. The quality has improved dramatically in the past 12 months. That said, you should always disclose AI-enhanced visuals in your listing materials — misrepresentation of property condition is a liability issue in most markets.

### How long does it take to make an AI real estate video?

With the tools in this article, expect 20–45 minutes for your first listing video while you're learning the workflow. After two or three videos, most agents I've talked to report completing a full listing narration video in 15–20 minutes. The bottleneck shifts from the tool to writing the script, which is where it should be.

### Are AI real estate videos good enough for MLS listings?

It depends heavily on the platform and the market. For social media — Instagram Reels, YouTube, Facebook — AI-generated listing videos are absolutely production-ready with any of the paid tiers in this review. For MLS listing pages specifically, check your local board's media guidelines. Some MLSs require that videos accurately represent the property, which means AI-staged visuals need to be disclosed or kept separate from the official listing photos. The narration-style videos (script + voiceover + stock footage) are generally fine for MLS marketing videos.

### Do I need video editing skills to use these tools?

No. All six tools in this review are designed for non-editors. Fliki is probably the most beginner-friendly — paste a script, pick a voice, review the auto-matched footage, export. The learning curve is less about skill and more about developing judgment for which AI outputs are good enough to use and which need a second take.
